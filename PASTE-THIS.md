# Investor Recon — the thing you paste

This is the free product. Open Claude Code, do the 5-minute setup once, paste the block, answer two questions. You get a grounded brief on the fund, built from their real public material, with sources. It will not make things up. If it cannot find something, it says so.

---

## Start here (5 minutes, one time)

1. **You need a paid Claude plan (Pro or higher).** Claude Code lives inside the Claude desktop app, and the Code tab will not open on free Claude. If you only have free Claude, this tool will not run.
2. **Get the app.** Download the Claude desktop app from claude.com/download, install it, and sign in. No terminal needed.
3. **Open the Code tab** at the top. If it asks you to upgrade, you are on free Claude and need a paid plan.
4. **Pick a folder.** Choose "Local", click "Select folder", and pick a new empty folder (make one on your Desktop called "recon"). That folder is where your briefs get saved. That is all it is for. (Windows only: you also need Git installed, from git-scm.com.)
5. **Web search is built in.** If it ever asks to enable web access, say yes. That is what lets it read the fund's real material instead of guessing.

That is the setup. You only do it once.

---

## Then paste this

Copy everything in the box and paste it into Claude Code. It will ask you two questions. Answer them.

```
You are my investor research analyst. Prep me for a VC meeting by building a grounded brief on a fund. Use real web search and fetch. This is the rule that matters: every claim about the fund must come from a source you actually read, and you must show me the link. If you cannot find something, say "Not found." Never guess the fund's deals, thesis, or check size from the name. If two sources disagree, show both. Keep what the fund SAYS separate from what their deals SUGGEST, and tell me which is which.

First, ask me:
1. Which fund am I meeting, and which partner if I know?
2. My company: name, what I do, my stage, and one or two traction facts. (You need this to find my weak spot.)

After I answer, do this:
- Search and read the fund's site, portfolio page, and any "how we invest" page. Quote their thesis, with links.
- Search and read the partner's essays, posts, or interviews. Tell me what they personally care about, in their words, with links.
- List the fund's recent investments you can actually confirm, each with a source. If you can't confirm them, say so.
- Map my company against their portfolio: where I fit, which portfolio company I collide with, the gap I fill.
- Predict the 8 questions this partner is most likely to ask me, and the shape of a strong answer to each.
- Tell me the single biggest hesitation THIS fund will have about MY company, in one sentence, the way the partner would say it to their team. Label this as your analysis, not fact.
- Tell me how to answer that hesitation before they raise it.

Then write it all as a one-page brief I can read in the lobby. End with a Sources list of every link you used, and a "Not found" list of anything you could not confirm.
```
