# Example: filled-in brief

This is what the system produces. The fund and company are invented. The shape is real.

Company: a seed-stage API security startup. Fund: a fictional early-stage B2B fund, "Northpath Capital." Partner: "Dana Reyes."

---

## Thesis brief (Prompt 1 output)

Northpath leads seed rounds in B2B infrastructure. Check size $1.5M to $3M, targets 12-15% ownership. Stated thesis is "developer-first security." Actual behavior is narrower: every recent deal sells to platform teams, not security teams. They passed on tools that sell to the CISO.

Dana writes often about "security that developers adopt without being told to." She rates bottom-up adoption over top-down sales.

Divergence flag: the homepage says "security." The portfolio says "developer tools that happen to be security." That gap matters for how you position.

## Portfolio map (Prompt 2 output)

Clean fit: you are developer-installed, no CISO sign-off needed. That is their pattern.

Collision: their portfolio company "Gatewise" does API gateway security. You overlap on API discovery. This is the live risk. Dana will see you as either a threat to Gatewise or a complement.

Gap you fill: none of their portfolio does runtime API behavior. That is your wedge.

## The hesitation (Prompt 4 output)

Dana to her team after the meeting: "Strong founder, but is this a feature Gatewise ships in two quarters rather than a company?"

## The counter (Prompt 5 output)

Surface it yourself: "You own Gatewise, so the obvious question is whether runtime behavior is just a Gatewise feature."

Answer: gateways see traffic at the edge. Runtime behavior needs to live in the workload. Different insertion point, different data, different buyer motion. Gatewise cannot ship this without re-architecting where they sit.

Do not say: "we're not really competitive with them." She knows the space. Denying the overlap reads as not understanding it.

## The room read (Prompt 6 output)

**They are confirming:** that you are a developer-adopted company, not a CISO-sales company, and that you are a platform not a Gatewise feature.

**Land these three:** bottom-up adoption proof, the runtime-vs-edge distinction, one metric that shows pull.

**Hesitation + counter:** "Feature not company." Counter: different insertion point, gateways can't reach runtime without re-architecting.

**Likely questions:** How do developers find you without a security mandate? What stops Gatewise from copying this? What is your install-to-active rate?

**Reference naturally:** Dana's post on "adoption you don't have to mandate." Your install motion is exactly that. Say it in her words.
