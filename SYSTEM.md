# The Recon System

Six prompts. Run them in order. Each one feeds the next.

Replace the `[BRACKETS]` with your inputs. Paste real material when asked: the fund's website text, the partner's posts or essays, your deck or one-pager.

Run all six in a single Claude session so the model keeps context between steps.

---

## Prompt 1 — Thesis brief

```
You are a research analyst building a dossier on a venture fund before a founder meeting.

Fund: [FUND NAME]
Partner I am meeting: [PARTNER NAME]
Here is their public material (website, about page, thesis posts): [PASTE]
Here is the partner's writing (essays, posts, interviews): [PASTE]
Here are their last 10-15 known investments: [PASTE OR LIST]

Tell me what this fund actually backs, based on behavior, not marketing copy.
- Stage they really lead at
- Sectors they repeat
- Typical check size and ownership target
- The pattern across their recent deals (what do the winners have in common)
- What the partner personally cares about, in their own words

Be specific. Cite the deal or the quote. Flag where the stated thesis and the actual behavior diverge.
```

---

## Prompt 2 — Portfolio map

```
Here is my company: [ONE-PARAGRAPH DESCRIPTION + STAGE + WHAT YOU DO]

Using the fund's portfolio from the previous step:
- Where do I fit their thesis cleanly?
- Which portfolio company, if any, do I collide with (competitive or overlapping)?
- What gap in their portfolio do I fill?
- Is there a portfolio company I could partner with or sell into? Name it.

If there is a conflict, say so plainly. A conflict can kill a deal and I need to know before the room.
```

---

## Prompt 3 — Question set

```
Based on this fund's thesis and my stage, list the 8-10 questions this partner is most likely to ask me.

Rank them by how likely and how sharp. For each one:
- The question, in the way this partner would actually phrase it
- What they are really testing with it
- The shape of a strong answer (not a script, the shape)

Skip generic VC questions. I want the ones specific to their thesis and my company.
```

---

## Prompt 4 — The hesitation

```
Every fund has one reason to pass on my company. Find mine, for this specific fund.

Given their thesis, their portfolio, their check size, and my stage and traction:
- What is the single biggest hesitation this fund will have about me?
- Why this fund specifically (not generic risk)?
- At what point in the meeting does it surface?

Give me one clear sentence: the hesitation as the partner would say it to their own team after the meeting.
```

---

## Prompt 5 — The counter

```
Take the hesitation from the previous step.

Write how I address it before they raise it. Not a deflection. A real answer that either removes the concern or reframes it as a strength.

Give me:
- The one line I say to surface it myself
- The evidence or framing that answers it
- What NOT to say (the defensive move that makes it worse)
```

---

## Prompt 6 — The room read

```
Synthesize everything into a one-page brief I read 10 minutes before the meeting.

Include:
- The thesis this fund is confirming about me when I walk in
- The 3 things I must land
- The hesitation and my counter, in one line each
- The 3 questions most likely to come, with the shape of my answer
- One specific thing about their portfolio or the partner to reference naturally (not forced)

Keep it to one page. I am reading it in the lobby.
```

---

## How to use the output

Read the brief once the night before. Read it again ten minutes before the meeting. Do not memorize it. The point is not a script. The point is that you walk in already knowing what they are thinking.

Run the whole thing fresh for each investor. The hesitation changes per fund. That is the entire point.
